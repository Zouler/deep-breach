# DEEP BREACH

**Story Canon Source of Truth**

Version 0.2 — Canon, immutable events, AI Story Director guardrails, submarine systems, RPG presence, and survival item expansion

# Purpose

This document defines the non-negotiable canon of Deep Breach. It exists to guide future writing, AI-generated side content, cutscenes, mission design, and Cursor prompts. The AI may react to player decisions, create side stories, dialogue, logs, images, and optional consequences, but it must not modify the main canon, inevitable events, character fundamentals, or the long-term escalation of the story.

# Canon Lock Box

- Main canon cannot be rewritten: AI-generated content can add side consequences, but cannot change the fixed campaign spine.
- The player is Commander Phillip Roberts: Roberts commands DBX-07, also known as Deep Breach.
- The game begins grounded: No aliens, invasion, or cosmic explanation should be revealed early.
- The ocean anomaly is the first mystery: It appears first as impossible distress signal, hull degradation, interference, and unexplained physical effects.
- The ending escalates: The anomaly eventually connects to global conflict, intergalactic war, civilizational collapse, and Roberts searching for survivors among ruins.

# High Concept

Deep Breach is a narrative command-survival RPG about commanding an experimental submarine through deep-sea missions, crew crises, military pressure, personal sacrifice, and a slow-burn mystery that eventually expands into planetary and intergalactic catastrophe.

| Axis | Canon Direction |
| --- | --- |
| Genre identity | Narrative command-survival RPG / submarine command drama |
| Core fantasy | Be the commander, not the pilot. Decide under pressure. |
| Tone | Military, tense, emotional, grounded sci-fi, slow-burn mystery |
| Core loop | Prepare expedition -> dive -> react to crises -> gather data/survive -> return/debrief -> upgrade/progress |
| Narrative promise | A simple recon mission becomes the first sign of something impossible growing beneath the ocean. |

| 12 | Aftermath | Roberts and remaining crew/DBX survivors search for people among the ruins, leaving room for a sequel or open ending. |

# Operation Dead Beacon - Canon Details

This is the first real story arc after the trials and the first proof that Deep Breach is more than a submarine sandbox.

| Element | Canon |
| --- | --- |
| Trigger | A distress signal matching DBX-03 authentication codes is detected near its last known loss zone. |
| Why impossible | DBX-03 was declared destroyed. It should have no active beacon, power source, or crew. |
| Mission framing | Limited reconnaissance only: confirm signal source, scan wreckage from safe distance, collect environmental readings, return. |
| Restriction | No recovery attempt authorized. |
| Initial assumption | The mission appears simple and safe enough for a newly operational Roberts and DBX-07. |
| Anomaly effects | Hull oxidation/degradation, reduced tolerance, sonar/radar interference, electrical failures, leaks, rising crew stress. |
| Key decision | Abort and Report vs Continue Approach. |
| Correct result | Abort and Report advances the story. Continuing leads to vessel loss and no story advancement. |
| Unlock | Hull Reinforcement Mk I / experimental hull treatment. |
| Second visit | DBX-07 returns, survives closer exposure, gathers anomaly data, and returns without full explanation. |

# AI Reactive Story Director Rules

The AI can generate reactive side content based on player choices, but must operate inside a strict sandbox. It should create the feeling that decisions echo through Roberts’ life, crew dynamics, and military consequences.

## Allowed AI Content

- Crew dialogue variations and cut-ins.
- Captain’s Log and XO recap entries.
- Side quests between missions.
- Family scenes and personal consequences.
- Crew conflicts, stress reactions, morale events.
- Military reprimands, commendations, or bureaucratic pressure.
- Anomaly flavor, hallucination-like incidents, false contacts, sensor confusion.
- Image prompts for cutscenes that respect canon and current era.
- Optional moral choices with small controlled effects.

## Forbidden AI Changes

- Cannot change the fixed campaign spine.
- Cannot reveal aliens or the true nature of the anomaly early.
- Cannot kill Roberts, destroy DBX-07 permanently, or end the campaign outside authored failure states.
- Cannot change mission success/failure rules, rewards, economy, inventory, unlocks, or death conditions.
- Cannot retcon DBX-03, DBX-07, Roberts, or the anomaly origin.
- Cannot create new canon family deaths, pregnancies, betrayals, or irreversible trauma without explicit authored approval.
- Cannot turn the story into comedy, fantasy, magic, superhero, or unrelated sci-fi.
- Cannot introduce explicit extraterrestrial contact before the late-game reveal gate.

# AI Output Validation Requirements

AI output should be structured, validated, and rejected if it violates canon, tone, safety gates, or gameplay balance.

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| type | dialogue, side_quest, captain_log, cutscene_prompt, crew_event, debriefing |
| canonImpact | must be none, low, or approved. Reject high impact without authored approval. |
| eraGate | must match current campaign era: trials, Dead Beacon, anomaly growth, war, collapse, aftermath. |
| revealLevel | must not exceed current reveal gate. |
| affectedStats | only small controlled effects; no direct arbitrary changes to hull/oxygen/death/unlocks. |
| choices | must map to existing consequence types or safe small modifiers. |
| imagePrompt | must obey current canon, character descriptions, era, and no early alien reveal. |
| fallbackId | must allow game to use authored fallback if output fails. |

# Tone and Style Rules

- Grounded first. The supernatural/cosmic feeling should emerge slowly through physical anomalies and incomplete data.
- Military language should be procedural, controlled, and tense.
- Roberts should feel responsible, not reckless by default. Player decisions can define his command style.
- Crew voices should be distinct and consistent.
- Family side stories should add emotional pressure, not soap-opera randomness.
- The ocean should feel like an active, unknowable presence before the true cause is revealed.
- Every AI scene should answer: what player decision caused this, and what emotional/gameplay consequence does it create?

# v0.2 Additions - Submarine Identity, Upgrade Fantasy, Items, and Roberts Presence

These additions are now part of the planning canon. They do not replace the fixed campaign spine. They define how DBX-07 should feel more like a real submarine, how it should upgrade over time, how ocean survival finds should expand, and how Roberts should become a more present RPG protagonist during and between missions.

# DBX-07 Submarine Identity Canon

- DBX-07 must feel like an experimental military submarine, not only a generic mobile resource panel.
- The vessel should have recognizable submarine-inspired systems: command/control, sonar, navigation, hull/ballast, propulsion, life support, engineering, communications, weapons, cargo, medical, crew quarters, research lab, security/brig, and external drone/recovery systems.
- DBX-07 is advanced and fictional, but its fantasy should be anchored in real submarine concepts: passive sonar, active sonar risk, masts/periscope-equivalent optics, ballast control, torpedo tubes, pressure hull, life support, communications limits, and compartment damage.
- The ship should upgrade as the campaign escalates: deeper diving, better anomaly resistance, better sensors, improved recovery capacity, stronger defensive/survival systems, and eventually military weapon systems.
- DBX-07 should never become a generic spaceship too early. The early and mid game must remain grounded naval sci-fi.

# Submarine-Inspired Systems to Add or Expand

## Priority Rooms / Compartments

- Command Center / Control Room: central decision space for Commander Roberts; shows mission status, command intent, alerts, navigation vector, current orders, and officer recommendations.
- Sonar Room: passive/active sonar, contact classification, signal tracking, anomaly interference, false contacts, signal ghosts, and later advanced acoustic analysis.
- Navigation Room: depth plotting, course setting, trench maps, thermocline layers, current drift, collision risk, and route planning.
- Engineering / Reactor or Power Bay: propulsion, battery/power stability, engine strain, overheating, blackout risk, emergency power routing.
- Hull / Ballast Control: ballast tanks, depth control, buoyancy emergencies, emergency blow, trim problems, crush depth risk, anomaly hull degradation.
- Life Support: oxygen generation/storage, CO2 scrubbers, air filtration, pressure regulation, contamination events, crew endurance.
- Communications Room: VLF/low-band communications, delayed orders, blackout zones, coded signals, distress beacons, corrupted transmissions.
- Optics Mast / Periscope Station: modern periscope-equivalent. Early game can use limited optical mast only near surface or shallow depth; later upgrades add infrared, low-light, thermal, anomaly filtering, and surface recon.
- Torpedo / Weapons Room: locked in early game. Later unlocks torpedoes, decoys, mines, anti-drone countermeasures, and possibly guided payloads. Weapons must arrive with story escalation, not in the tutorial.
- Research Lab: anomaly samples, DBX wreckage analysis, relic/artifact processing, biological readings, material decay studies, signal archaeology.
- Cargo / Recovery Bay: salvage storage, ROV/drone deployment, external clamps, sample containers, pressure-safe lockers.
- Medical Bay: injuries, decompression-like events, stress symptoms, sedation, triage after accidents, family/crew side quest consequences.
- Security / Brig: CSO domain. Handles contraband, crew fights, sabotage, survivors/prisoners, restricted access, isolation protocols.
- Crew Quarters / Mess: morale, fatigue, interpersonal conflict, rumors, family messages, rest cycles, stress recovery.

## Priority Equipment / Upgrade Tracks

- Sonar Suite: passive hydrophones, active ping, side-scan mapping, towed array equivalent, anomaly filter, contact classifier, signal triangulation.
- Optics Mast: replaces the old-school periscope fantasy with a modern photonics/optronics mast. Upgrades can add low-light, infrared, thermal, laser range, and signal overlay.
- Hull Reinforcement: pressure tolerance, anomaly degradation resistance, emergency sealant compatibility, deeper operational depth.
- Ballast and Trim Systems: faster emergency ascent/descent correction, reduced depth instability, improved survival during flooding.
- Propulsion: engine strain tolerance, silent running, pump-jet equivalent, emergency thrust, efficiency under pressure.
- Power Systems: battery reserves, emergency power, blackout resistance, rerouting speed, sensor uptime during failures.
- Life Support: oxygen capacity, CO2 scrubbing, contamination resistance, crew endurance, emergency oxygen distribution.
- Communications: better reception at depth, distress signal decoding, delayed command packets, encrypted Navy orders.
- Recovery Systems: ROV, manipulator arm, magnetic clamps, pressure sample capsules, deep cargo pod, external drone.
- Weapons: torpedo tubes, acoustic decoys, mines, interceptor drones, later cruise-missile or special payload systems if the campaign reaches open war. Weapon unlocks must be story-gated.

# Sensor Gameplay Rules

- Passive sonar should be safer and stealthier, but slower and less precise.
- Active sonar should give stronger returns but can reveal DBX-07, agitate anomalies, increase stress, or trigger hostile attention in later acts.
- Radar should be mostly surface/periscope-depth relevant, not a magic deep-ocean sensor. Deep detection fantasy should rely on sonar, signal analysis, and anomaly instruments.
- Optics mast/periscope should be limited by depth and tactical situation; it is powerful for surface confirmation, visual cutscenes, and shallow recon, not constant deep exploration.
- Sensor upgrades should create better gameplay choices: listen silently, ping actively, launch ROV, deploy towed array, use anomaly filter, or risk surface/mast exposure.

# Weapons Canon and Progression Rules

- Weapons are allowed in Deep Breach, but they should not dominate early game.
- Early game: survival, research, reconnaissance, repairs, mystery.
- Mid game: defensive countermeasures, decoys, emergency torpedoes, hostile naval pressure.
- Late game: full military escalation, naval war, intergalactic conflict, and special payloads tied to the collapse arc.
- The first weapons should feel like submarine systems: torpedoes, acoustic decoys, mines, countermeasure pods, anti-drone/anti-UUV payloads.
- Any weapon use should create consequences: noise, escalation, Navy scrutiny, ammunition scarcity, collateral risk, crew stress, and mission branching.
- AI-generated side content cannot unlock major weapons or start wars. Weapon unlocks are canon/story-gated only.

# Expanded Ocean Survival and Salvage Item Taxonomy

Current survival finds are too limited. The ocean should produce varied, useful, strange, and story-relevant recoveries. Items should be grouped by gameplay function so rewards are easy to balance.

## Repair and Hull Items

- Hull Patch Kit: repairs direct hull damage.
- Pressure Sealant: seals leaks and microfractures.
- Structural Resin: slower but stronger hull stabilization.
- Titanium Plate Scrap: crafting/repair material for hull upgrades.
- Ceramic Pressure Tile: rare hull reinforcement component.
- Anomaly-Resistant Coating Sample: research/upgrade component after Dead Beacon.

## Life Support Items

- Oxygen Canister: emergency oxygen recovery.
- CO2 Scrubber Cartridge: reduces air toxicity events.
- Air Filter Pack: counters contamination/smoke events.
- Medical Oxygen Mask: crew injury/emergency event support.
- Pressure Suit Patch: protects external/recovery teams in future missions.

## Power and Engineering Items

- Battery Cell: restores emergency power or supports blackout recovery.
- Capacitor Bank: improves sensor burst or emergency systems.
- Coolant Canister: reduces engine/reactor overheating.
- Fuse Assembly: repairs electrical failures.
- Motor Coil: propulsion repair/upgrade component.
- Pump Assembly: ballast/flooding repair component.

## Sensor and Communications Items

- Hydrophone Module: sonar repair or passive detection upgrade.
- Signal Amplifier: improves weak beacon detection.
- Encrypted Data Core: story/research item from wrecks.
- Antenna Segment: communications repair or VLF upgrade.
- Sonar Calibration Disc: improves contact classification.
- Corrupted Beacon Recorder: anomaly/DBX wreck clue.

## Research / Anomaly Items

- Unknown Alloy Fragment: research item tied to DBX wrecks/anomaly.
- Bioluminescent Sample: ocean research and environmental storytelling.
- Pressure Fossil: rare research value and lore flavor.
- Anomalous Silt: early clue that the ocean floor is changing.
- Magnetic Stone / Metallic Nodule: sensor interference and upgrade material.
- Non-Human Geometry Relic: late-gated item; must not reveal aliens early.

## Crew and Morale Items

- Ration Crate: improves crew morale or extends expedition endurance.
- Coffee / Stimulant Pack: short-term crew readiness with fatigue consequence.
- Personal Letter Bundle: triggers crew/RPG side content.
- Old Photograph: emotional log/cutscene item.
- Music Tape / Audio Recorder: morale, Roberts memory, or crew event.
- Religious Charm / Lucky Token: morale flavor, no magic effects.

## Weapons and Countermeasure Items

- Torpedo Parts: late/mid-game weapons maintenance.
- Acoustic Decoy Canister: distracts threats or reduces detection.
- Mine Component: later war-era unlock.
- Guidance Chip: torpedo or drone upgrade component.
- Countermeasure Foam: reduces sonar lock / interference effect.
- Explosive Charge: restricted item; CSO/security event risk.

## Salvage Rarity Rules

- Common: scrap, basic repair items, oxygen, fuses, filters.
- Uncommon: hydrophone modules, coolant, structural resin, battery cells, signal amplifiers.
- Rare: data cores, unknown alloy, ceramic pressure tiles, beacon recorders, anomaly samples.
- Very rare: unique relics, DBX prototype fragments, classified Navy cargo, late-game non-human materials.
- Search for Salvage should increase common/uncommon survival item odds; Follow Signal should increase data/sensor item odds; Anomaly missions should increase research items but also risk.

# Roberts Presence and RPG Role-Playing Canon

Roberts must stop disappearing once a mission begins. The player should feel like they are role-playing Commander Phillip Roberts, not only pressing system buttons.

## Roberts Must Be Present Through

- Commander thoughts: short internal monologue during critical choices, mission starts, losses, discoveries, and returns to base.
- Command stance choices: Roberts can respond as cautious, aggressive, compassionate, procedural, skeptical, or obsessed. These should affect tone, crew trust, family pressure, and Navy reputation in small controlled ways.
- Personal log: Roberts writes or dictates after missions; AI can generate variations within canon.
- Family pressure: Roberts has a family life that creates emotional stakes, but AI cannot create irreversible family tragedy without authored approval.
- Crew relationship: officers should react to Roberts’ pattern of decisions over time.
- Psychological state: stress, guilt, fatigue, obsession, command burden. These should appear as flavor and light modifiers, not as arbitrary punishment.
- Visual presence: portraits/cut-ins of Roberts in command chair, quarters, debriefing, hospital/family scenes, funeral/failure variants, and aging over the campaign.

## Suggested Roberts RPG Stats / Tracks

- Command Reputation: how Navy Command sees Roberts.
- Crew Trust: how much the crew believes Roberts will bring them home.
- Family Strain: pressure from missed time, danger, trauma, and war escalation.
- Psychological Stress: accumulated burden from deaths, failures, anomaly exposure, and impossible decisions.
- Obsession / Anomaly Pull: late-game track reflecting Roberts becoming increasingly tied to the mystery.
- Compassion vs Duty: not a morality meter, but a memory of repeated role-play choices.

## Roberts Choice Style Examples

- Cautious: “We came for data, not a coffin. Pull us back.”
- Aggressive: “Hold course. I want another scan before we run.”
- Compassionate: “Seal the section and get the injured out first. The data can wait.”
- Procedural: “Log the anomaly, transmit the packet, and follow withdrawal protocol.”
- Obsessive: “Something woke that beacon. We are not leaving blind.”

## AI Reactive Story Director Rules for Roberts

- AI can generate Roberts internal thoughts, personal log entries, family side quests, and crew reactions.
- AI must base Roberts scenes on actual player actions, not random melodrama.
- AI cannot force Roberts to make choices the player did not make.
- AI cannot rewrite Roberts as cowardly, evil, comedic, supernatural, or incompetent unless the player’s actual decisions justify criticism from other characters.
- AI cannot permanently kill family members, destroy Roberts’ family, or create irreversible trauma without explicit authored approval.
- AI should make Roberts feel more human: fear, responsibility, guilt, love, obsession, exhaustion, and leadership under pressure.

# Research Notes - Real Submarine Concepts Adapted for Deep Breach

- Modern submarines rely heavily on passive and active sonar. Active sonar gives a stronger signal but can reveal the submarine’s presence, which maps directly into a useful risk/reward gameplay choice.
- Modern periscope fantasy can be updated as a photonics/optronics mast: cameras/sensors on a mast instead of a traditional optical tube. This supports visual cutscenes, surface confirmation, infrared/low-light upgrades, and better command-room screens.
- Submarine torpedo tubes are general-purpose launchers and can support torpedoes and, in some designs, other payloads. For Deep Breach, this supports a later weapons/countermeasure room without turning early game into combat.
- Real submarine operations include control/navigation, sonar, propulsion/power, ballast/depth control, life support, communications, torpedo/weapons spaces, crew spaces, and damage control. These can be translated into rooms and upgrade tracks.
